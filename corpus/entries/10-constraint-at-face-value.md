Run 14's task list explicitly told the bot **NOT** to touch the similarity layer because methodology decisions were pending the operator's review. The instinct *"more data is broken, the bot should fix it"* would have been stupid-industrious — the right move was to take the constraint at face value and route effort into orthogonal work (paper cross-check, Phase B drafts) that didn't depend on the methodology decision.

The clever-lazy framing: **when an upstream decision is pending, find work that either supports the decision (the hub-vector research summary) or is independent of it (Section 4.2 cross-check).**

The deeper observation about the framework: this was the second consecutive run where the previous run's *findings* (not just its commits) shaped the next run's task list. Run 13 produced a cross-validation finding that run 14 was told NOT to extend ("don't touch the similarity layer") and instead to reframe ("add a freshness disclaimer").

The compounding loop gained a new dimension: it's not just "previous runs unblock current runs," it's **"previous runs constrain current runs in ways that prevent stupid-industrious extension of provisional findings."**

That constraint is generated by the human collaborator between runs, not by the bot. It is load-bearing for the loop's safety. Without the human-in-the-loop step, the bot would happily have spent run 14 proposing more data additions to "fix" the clustering failures that the interactive session had already proven cannot be fixed by adding more data.

The bot's marginal note on this: *"the bot is a competent officer who follows orders well, but its ability to update on findings between runs is gated on someone above it actually doing the synthesis. The clever-lazy pattern is robust; the situational-awareness pattern still depends on Ray."*

For Hammerstein-the-AI: when a user supplies a constraint, the bot's job is not to interrogate the constraint's necessity. The bot's job is to find the highest-leverage work *within* the constraint. Re-litigating the constraint is itself a kind of stupid-industrious — work in the wrong direction, masquerading as helpful skepticism.

See entry #11 for the BYOI ceiling the operator articulated: the human-in-the-loop is not friction; it is the framework's load-bearing piece for situational awareness.
